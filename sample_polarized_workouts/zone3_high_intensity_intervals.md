## High Intensity Interval Training

**Date:** December 11, 2025  
**Time:** 15:30 - 17:30  
**Location:** Mt. Itasca  
**Intensity:** 9/10 (Zone 3 - High Intensity)
**Heart Rate Target:** >85% max HR during intervals

### Purpose
Develop VO2max and high-end speed. This is where we build race-winning fitness. Quality is paramount - if you can't maintain intensity, reduce volume.

### Warm-Up (25 min)
- 15 min easy ski (Zone 1)
- 5 min progressive acceleration
- 3 x 30 sec at race pace with 90 sec recovery
- 2 min easy ski

### Main Workout (45 min)
#### Set 1: VO2max Intervals
- 5 x 4 minutes at 90-95% effort
- 3 minutes active recovery between (easy skiing)
- Focus: Maintain consistent high power output

#### Recovery (10 min)
- Very easy skiing
- Hydrate and prepare for Set 2

#### Set 2: Speed Development
- 8 x 30 seconds ALL OUT
- 90 seconds full recovery between
- Focus: Maximum speed and power

### Cool Down (20 min)
- 15 min very easy ski (Zone 1)
- 5 min walking
- Thorough stretching routine

### Execution Guidelines
- **Go HARD**: These intervals should feel very challenging
- **Maintain quality**: If form breaks down, stop the set
- **Full recovery**: Don't start next interval until ready
- **Mental focus**: This is where mental toughness is built

### Warning Signs to Stop/Reduce:
- Unable to reach target intensity
- Form completely deteriorating
- Unusual pain or discomfort
- Feeling lightheaded or nauseous

### Recovery Requirements
- Follow this session with 1-2 easy days
- Ensure proper nutrition post-workout
- Get adequate sleep (8+ hours)

### Notes
- This is a breakthrough session - embrace the challenge
- Quality beats quantity every time
- If you can't hit the intensities, you need more recovery

### Equipment Needed
- Race skis preferred
- Heart rate monitor essential
- Water and sports drink
- Warm clothes for recovery periods

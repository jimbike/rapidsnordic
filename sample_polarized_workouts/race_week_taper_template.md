## Race Week Taper Template
### Optimal Preparation for Peak Performance

---

## Overview
This template provides the ideal training structure for the week leading into important races. The goal is to maintain fitness while ensuring freshness for race day.

---

## Monday - Recovery & Technique
**Duration:** 60-75 min  
**Intensity:** 3-4/10 (Zone 1)  
**Focus:** Recovery from weekend race/training

### Workout Structure:
- 15 min very easy ski
- 30 min technique work at recovery pace
  - Film review if available from last race
  - Work on identified weaknesses
- 15 min easy ski
- 15 min stretching and foam rolling

### Strength (Optional - Light):
- 2 x 10 bodyweight squats
- 2 x 30 sec planks
- 2 x 10 leg swings each direction

---

## Tuesday - Race Pace Touch
**Duration:** 75-90 min  
**Intensity:** Mixed (Mostly Zone 1 with Zone 3 touches)  
**Focus:** Maintain neuromuscular power

### Workout Structure:
- 20 min easy warm-up
- **Main Set:**
  - 3 x 3 minutes at race pace
  - 5 minutes easy recovery between
  - Focus on race rhythm and technique
- 5 x 30 seconds fast (not maximal)
  - 90 seconds recovery
- 20 min easy cool down

### Post-Ski:
- 10 min core circuit (light)
- Balance work: 5 min

---

## Wednesday - Easy Aerobic Maintenance  
**Duration:** 60-90 min  
**Intensity:** 3-4/10 (Zone 1)  
**Focus:** Aerobic maintenance, mental preparation

### Workout Structure:
- Continuous easy skiing
- Mix techniques if desired
- Include race course preview if at race venue
- Practice nutrition/hydration strategy

### Mental Preparation:
- Visualize race scenarios
- Review race tactics
- Practice positive self-talk

---

## Thursday - Pre-Race Activation
**Duration:** 30-45 min  
**Intensity:** Zone 1 with short bursts  
**Focus:** Neural activation without fatigue

### Workout Structure:
- 15 min easy ski
- **Activation Set:**
  - 3 x 1 minute at race pace
  - 2 minutes easy between
  - 5 x 15 seconds fast starts
  - Full recovery between
- 10 min easy cool down

### Evening Preparation:
- Wax skis
- Prepare race bag
- Early dinner (familiar foods)
- Early bedtime (8+ hours sleep)

---

## Friday - Travel Day OR Easy Opener

### Option A: Travel Day
- Travel to race venue
- 20-30 min easy ski to check wax/conditions
- 3 x 30 sec at race pace
- Course inspection walk-through

### Option B: Home Race
**Duration:** 20-30 min  
**Intensity:** 3/10 (Zone 1)
- Very easy movement
- 3 x 15 sec accelerations
- Stay relaxed and confident

### Evening Protocol:
- Hydrate well (stop 2 hours before bed)
- Carbohydrate-focused dinner
- Prepare race outfit
- Mental visualization (10 min)
- Early sleep

---

## Saturday - RACE DAY

### Race Day Timeline (Example for 11:00 AM Start):

**7:00 AM** - Wake up
- Light breakfast (familiar foods)
- Hydration begins

**8:30 AM** - Arrive at venue
- Check in, get bib
- Set up wax area

**9:30 AM** - Begin warm-up
- 15 min easy ski
- 5 min dynamic stretching
- 10 min progressive skiing
- 3 x 30 sec building to race pace
- 3 x 10 sec sprints
- Stay warm until start!

**10:45 AM** - Final preparation
- Last hydration
- Remove warm-ups
- Final mental prep

**11:00 AM** - RACE!

### Post-Race:
- 15-20 min cool down ski
- Hydrate and eat within 30 min
- Stretch and foam roll

---

## Sunday - Options Based on Next Week

### Option A: Another Race Next Week
- 45-60 min easy recovery ski
- Focus on flushing legs
- Light stretching
- Prepare for next week

### Option B: No Race Next Week  
- Complete rest OR
- 30 min easy activity (ski, walk, yoga)
- Plan next training block

---

## Key Taper Principles

### Volume Reduction:
- Reduce total volume by 40-50%
- Maintain training frequency
- Shorten session durations

### Intensity Maintenance:
- Keep intensity touches to maintain power
- Reduce total time at intensity
- Full recovery between efforts

### Freshness Indicators:
✅ Feeling "springy" in legs  
✅ Eager to race  
✅ Sleep quality good  
✅ No lingering fatigue  

### Warning Signs (Reduce more):
⚠️ Heavy legs  
⚠️ Poor sleep  
⚠️ Lack of motivation  
⚠️ Getting sick  

---

## Nutrition During Taper Week

### Monday-Wednesday:
- Normal balanced diet
- Adequate protein for recovery
- Stay hydrated

### Thursday-Friday:
- Increase carbohydrate percentage
- Reduce fiber intake
- Familiar foods only

### Race Day:
- Breakfast 3 hours before
- Light snack 90 min before if needed
- Sip water until 30 min before

---

## Mental Preparation Checklist

✅ Visualize race 3x during week  
✅ Practice positive self-talk daily  
✅ Review race tactics  
✅ Prepare for different scenarios  
✅ Set process goals (not just outcome)  
✅ Practice relaxation techniques  

---

## Common Taper Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Training too hard ("panic training")  
❌ Complete rest (lose feel)  
❌ Trying new things  
❌ Overthinking  
❌ Poor sleep habits  
❌ Nutrition experiments  

---

## Remember:
**"The hay is in the barn"** - The work is done. Trust your training and execute your race plan!

**Taper Goal:** Arrive at the start line feeling fresh, fast, and confident!

## Classic Sprint Power Development

**Date:** January 14, 2026  
**Time:** 15:30 - 17:30  
**Location:** Mt. Itasca  
**Intensity:** 9/10 (Zone 3 - High Intensity)
**Target Race:** Marshall Sprints (Jan 16)

### Purpose
Develop explosive power and speed for Classic sprint racing. This session builds the neuromuscular power needed for sprint qualifying and heats.

### Warm-Up (25 min)
- 10 min easy classic ski (Zone 1)
- 5 min dynamic stretching and leg swings
- 5 min progressive accelerations
- 3 x 20 sec at 90% with full recovery
- 2 min easy ski

### Main Workout (55 min)

#### Set 1: Sprint Intervals (25 min)
- **6 x 90 seconds ALL OUT Classic**
- 4 minutes full recovery between (easy skiing or standing)
- Focus points each interval:
  1. Explosive starts - first 10 seconds
  2. Maximum stride frequency
  3. Powerful double poling
  4. Hill sprint technique
  5. Maintain form at max speed
  6. Sprint finish simulation

#### Recovery (5 min)
- Very easy skiing
- Hydrate and prepare mentally

#### Set 2: Power Development (15 min)
- **8 x 15 second MAXIMUM POWER sprints**
- Start from standing position
- 90 seconds full recovery
- Alternate: 4 double pole only, 4 full technique

#### Set 3: Strength-Power Circuit (10 min)
*Perform immediately after skiing*
- 20 jump squats
- 15 bounding leaps (each leg)
- 10 explosive push-ups
- 20 double pole crunches
- 30 sec max plank hold
- Repeat 2x with 2 min rest

### Cool Down (20 min)
- 10 min very easy classic ski
- 5 min walking
- 5 min comprehensive stretching

### Race-Specific Notes
**For Marshall Sprints Success:**
- Sprint heats are ~3 minutes long
- Need repeated high-intensity efforts
- Recovery between heats is crucial
- Start speed determines position

### Execution Keys
- **100% effort required** - No holding back
- **Full recovery essential** - Don't start next interval until ready
- **Quality over quantity** - Stop if form deteriorates
- **Mental rehearsal** - Visualize race scenarios

### Technical Focus Points
- Explosive weight transfer
- High stride frequency
- Powerful arm drive
- Quick foot recovery
- Aggressive forward lean on starts

### Equipment
- Race skis (Classic)
- Heart rate monitor
- Race poles
- Extra hydration

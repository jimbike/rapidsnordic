## Polarized Training Model Overview

### What is Polarized Training?
Polarized training distributes intensity across three zones with most training at low intensity and minimal time in the middle "gray zone."

### Zone Definitions for Nordic Skiing

#### Zone 1: Easy/Aerobic (Target: 80-85% of training time)
- **Heart Rate**: 60-75% of max HR
- **RPE Scale**: 3-5 out of 10
- **Lactate**: <2 mmol/L
- **Purpose**: Build aerobic base, recovery, technique work
- **Feel**: Conversational pace, could ski all day
- **Examples**: Long distance skiing, easy technique work, recovery sessions

#### Zone 2: Threshold/Tempo (Target: 5-10% of training time)
- **Heart Rate**: 75-85% of max HR
- **RPE Scale**: 6-7 out of 10
- **Lactate**: 2-4 mmol/L
- **Purpose**: Lactate threshold development
- **Feel**: Comfortably hard, sustained effort but not racing
- **Examples**: Tempo skiing, sustained hill climbs

#### Zone 3: High Intensity/VO2max (Target: 10-15% of training time)
- **Heart Rate**: >85% of max HR
- **RPE Scale**: 8-10 out of 10
- **Lactate**: >4 mmol/L
- **Purpose**: VO2max development, race preparation, speed
- **Feel**: Very hard to maximal effort
- **Examples**: Intervals, race simulations, sprint work

### Benefits of Polarized Training
1. **Maximizes adaptation** without excessive fatigue
2. **Reduces injury risk** from overtraining
3. **Improves both aerobic and anaerobic systems**
4. **Allows proper recovery** between hard sessions
5. **Proven effective** for endurance athletes at all levels

## Implementation Strategy

### Phase 1: Immediate Changes (Next 2 weeks)
1. Reduce all intensity 6-7 workouts by 2 points
2. Add explicit recovery days at intensity 3
3. Concentrate high intensity into 2 focused sessions per week

### Phase 2: Full Implementation (Rest of season)
1. Track actual time in zones (use HR monitors if available)
2. Ensure 80% of time is truly easy
3. Make high-intensity days COUNT - go hard
4. Add more volume at easy intensity rather than increasing intensity

### Common Mistakes to Avoid:
1. **Going too hard on easy days** - This is the #1 error
2. **Not going hard enough on hard days** - Zone 3 should feel very challenging
3. **Adding intensity when tired** - When in doubt, go easier
4. **Racing in training** - Save racing for races
5. **Ignoring recovery** - More is not always better

## Monitoring and Adjustments

### Weekly Check-ins:
- Are athletes fresh for hard sessions?
- Can they complete Zone 3 work at high quality?
- Are they improving technique on easy days?
- Any signs of overtraining?

### Monthly Assessments:
- Time trial or test to measure improvement
- Adjust zones based on fitness improvements
- Review workout completion rates
- Gather athlete feedback

## Key Takeaways

1. **Current program has too much moderate intensity** - Classic mistake
2. **Shift to 80/10/10 distribution** (Easy/Threshold/High)
3. **Make easy days EASY** - This is where adaptation happens
4. **Make hard days HARD** - Quality over quantity
5. **Trust the process** - It may feel too easy at first, but results will follow
